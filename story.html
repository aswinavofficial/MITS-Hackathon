The Story

Engineers need to be the artists of the future, creatively solving problems of complex systems, environments, and cultures in a globally connected world. Many of the challenges that we face in the world today can be addressed only by the implementation of innovative engineering solutions, a fact indubitably acknowledged by industry and academia. Yet at this time when our need for engineering talent is huge, our country is clearly lagging behind in producing quality engineers with necessary employability skills. This can be attributed mostly to the huge gap existing between industry and academia. A simple curricular solution cannot address adequately this profound challenge. Rather, the stake holders from academia, industry and research need to form discursive alliances, based on mutual respect, that will enrich understanding and create the basis for meaningful deliberations. On a similar note, Muthoot Institute of Technology and Science is organizing an ‘ Employability Conclave ’ on 16/03/2018,wherein some of the best minds from industry and academia is coming together under the same roof for sharing some interesting insights on "From Qualification based to Skill Based" - The Half- Life of Knowledge and changing employability scenario”. Parallel to this the annual flagship event, ‘ MITS HACKATHON ’, a 3-day programming and problem solving challenge, will happen wherein we expect the best engineering minds from all-over India putting into test their core engineering skills. We believe the event will be a comprehensive evaluation of their Complex problem solving skills, Creativity, Critical thinking, Collaboration and Communication.
