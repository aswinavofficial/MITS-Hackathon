FAQs

  • I’ve registered, now what?
    Thank you for registering. Just before closing the registrations, we’ll contact you via email with the entry confirmation. We will shortlist the teams based on your skills and the achievements.
  • What happens if my team gets shortlisted?
    If you get shortlisted, we’ll get in touch with you over phone and email to make sure you know about that in time. You’ll receive an invite for the event and a dedicated contact person to reach out to with all your queries.
  • What is going to happen at the hackathon?
    We will arrange for you 3 days of excitement, fun and everything else you’d need to convert your idea into an application / prototype. You can build a web/mobile app based on a concept / idea you have on the given theme. Even if you can't think of an idea right away, that's fine. Our team will reach out to you with a list of problem statements that you can optionally use, to build an idea on. After all, it’s not the idea that matters but what you do with it.

  • What do you mean by this theme?
    As artificial intelligence, machine learning, robotics, IoT and other disruptive technologies are going to take over our future, we believe that bringing in an element of ‘Smartness’ through technology to our lives, makes a lot of sense. This smart world is set to be the next important stage in human history. MITS HACKATHON aims at investigating on the essential problems and crucial issues in truly building the smart world that benefits humanity, and simultaneously safeguards the natural environment for sustainable development and evolution.
  • What is the plan of action of this event?
    In each of the broad realms of human concern — sustainability, health, vulnerability, education, transportation, energy, infrastructure, joy of living — specific grand challenges await engineering solutions. The bright minds competing in MITS hackathon will seek ways to put knowledge into practice to meet these grand challenges. Applying the rules of reason, the findings of science, the aesthetics of art, and the spark of creative imagination, we expect them to bring in innovative and creative engineering solutions bringing in an element of ‘SMARTNESS THROUGH TECHNOLOGY’.
  • Who can attend?
    The contest is open for students currently doing undergraduate engineering (BE/B.Tech) or Post graduate Engineering (ME/M.Tech) or MCA in any of the AICTE approved institutions in India. The contest is also open for students passed out in 2017. All you need is an interest to build something from scratch , based on the theme provided. Even if you don’t have an idea don’t worry – we’ll help you ideate.
  • How many team members can be in one group?
    Maximum team size will be limited to 4.
  • Do all team members have to register?
    One member can register. While registering, you have to provide all the details of your team as per the form. Registration fees can either through online or offline.
  • Do I need to bring my laptop and other device(s)?
    Yes, you need to bring your own devices.
  • What technologies can I code on?
    Anything you’re comfortable with.
  • Will there be food and accomodation?
    Absolutely! Your meals for the weekend are on the house and there’ll be plenty of snacks and drinks to keep you energized. Also, accomodation will be provided for the participants in the college hostel.
  • Will I annoy people asking questions?
    No, the environment at these events are designed specifically to encourage everyone to have fun and help each other. If you’re ever stuck on anything, please find a volunteer and he/she will help you. We also encourage participating teams to help each other.
  • What if I don’t want to present my hack?
    You might think your project isn’t impressive or it might not be finished so you don’t want to present it. You should present it anyway! Honestly, hackathons aren’t about being the best or being competitive. It’s all about having fun, making friends and learning how to become better at things you care about. Presenting your hack gives you a chance to be proud of what you’ve done, and you should be proud.
  • How is this hackathon judged?
    You need that to be presenting a working prototype of what you developed. The evaluation panel will consist of representatives from industry, academia and research. We expect a comprehensive evaluation of the important 21st century engineering skills. We also will be doing a quantitative mapping to the 12 Graduate attributes/ POs, consistent with the theme we follow here in MITS- ‘Engineer as a PO’. The evaluation sheets with specific value points for evaluating 21st century engineering skills,Coding Skills,PO mappings will be used.
  • What do I get?
    While you get to win a hefty cash prize, learn and network, we believe the most important take-away is the experience itself. Being part of technology events like these, gives you a chance to polish your skills, network, gain insight into new technologies and identify opportunities.  You also get the opportunity to be eligible for an internship or career with our any of the partners.
